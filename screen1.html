Step 1.

Data Harvesting

Goal: Gather raw material to make the scam believable.


Scammers collect:

  • Voice clips (social media videos, WhatsApp audios)
  • Photos/videos (Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook)
  • Personal info (job, family, phone number)
  • Leaked data (emails, passwords, bank info)
  • Organizational details (who reports to whom)

AI's role: Helps scrape, organize, and profile victims fast. Even tiny data points become powerful when AI stitches them together.


Without data, there is no personalization. Without personalization, AI scams lose power.

Authority Fabrication

Scammer claims institutional power.


Examples: police, bank official, tax officer, courier, telecom, HR.

Distress Manipulation

Scammer performs vulnerability to trigger rescue instinct.


Examples: stranded traveler, medical emergency plea, charity fraud.

Affinity Exploitation

Scammer builds perceived similarity or attraction.


Examples: romance scams, investment "insider" groups, shared community/religion, alumni networks.

Identity Hijacking

Scammer impersonates someone already trusted.


Examples: brother in emergency, CEO asking for transfer, friend requesting OTP.

Step 3.

Emotional triggers

Scammers engineer isolation to intensify urgency, pushing victims to act before doubt or outside perspective can intervene.

Fear

“You are under investigation and a warrant will be issued if you do not act immediately.”

Your Aadhaar has been found to be linked to  illegal activity. Press 1 to avoid suspension.

Desperation

“You were selected from 10,000 applicants for this job role.”

“We can fast-track your visa application if payment is made immediately.”

FOMO

“This pre-IPO allocation is invite-only. I shouldn’t even be sharing this.”

“Two buyers are already negotiating. If you’re serious, we need a token today.”

Obligation

“We are raising emergency funds for people affected by the earthquake.”

“I’ve lost my wallet, can you please help me pay my bus fee.”

Curiosity

“Is this you in this video?”

“Unusual login attempt detected. Review activity now.”

Skepticism should be your default operating mode.

Verify identity of caller through a second, independent, and trusted channel.

Treat time as a warning signal if an interaction has stretched unusually long.

Refuse real-time compliance.

In moments of distress or urgency, seek an external perspective.

Reject sunk cost thinking.

Separate emotional intimacy from financial or informational access.

Check for isolation language: “don’t tell anyone,” “act now,” “only you.”

Search the exact phrasing of the request online. Scammer scripts repeat.

Impose a mandatory delay before any transfer or document sharing. Legitimate needs withstand delays.

Never share passwords under any circumstance.

If money is involved, use only official institutional channels. Never links or alternate payment methods.

Trust built earlier is irrelevant at the moment of extraction. Reassess the request from zero.
